Our story

Where Discovery Day began.

When Peterson Middle School lost science funding in 2025, Discovery Day was founded to fill the gap: free, hands-on science for students who might otherwise never get it. The first year was three classes and 108 students, proof that one good experiment can change how a kid sees science.

After Kimi Yashar founded Discovery Day in 2025, she brought on two avid science enthusiasts, Shivaani Kumara Venkatesh and Deniz Ertem, to keep bringing Discovery Days to more students.

Passing it on

Built to last.

To keep Discovery Day going, two Harker freshmen were recruited to take it on. Now sophomores, they lead the program: designing new labs, working with teachers, and running Discovery Days of their own, like the two-day greenhouse workshop that reached 330 students this spring.

What we believe

Curiosity, not circumstance.

🔬Wonder first

We lead with the "wow" moment, the spark that turns a curious kid into someone who wants to understand how the world works.

⚖️Equity in science

We focus on under-resourced schools, so access to real science is driven by curiosity, not by a school's budget.

🌎Every learner

Every class has equal parts images, visual instructions, explanations, and lessons, so everyone can follow along no matter how they learn best.
